Cashew Queso

Ubiquitous in ballparks and Tex-Mex restaurants everywhere is the glorious combination of hot, liquefied cheese and crispy tortilla chips. The quality (and moniker) of said dish varies wildly from venue to venue, but the cheese type product utilized hovers at a near constant level: industrially produced yellow gloop.

Upgrade from the dismal experience of mysterious dairy products and say ‘Right on!’ to the rich, creamy goodness of blended cashews seasoned to delicious perfection. Did we mention it's vegan and gluten-free?

Ingredients

  • .75 cup cashews, soaked for at least 2 hours (Review Cashew Tips and Tricks)
  • 1.25 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 Tb white miso paste
  • 3 Tb nutritional yeast flakes
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • .25 tsp salt
  • 2 TB fresh lemon juice

  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and chopped

Process

  1. Drain cashews and add them to a blender along with the vegetable broth, miso, nutritional yeast, garlic, cumin, salt, and lemon juice. Puree until completely smooth, which may take several minutes depending on your blender (I recommend investing in a Vitamix for the most extreme blending pleasure).
  2. Preheat a 2-quart saucepan over low heat and add the oil. Sauté the onion and jalapeño for 3 minutes, or until just soft.
  3. Add blended cashew mixture to saucepan and increase heat to medium.
  4. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally until the queso is hot and thickened.
  5. Keep warm until ready to dig in!

This recipe makes a wonderful companion to Lentil Tacos and Triple Bean Super Chili, but we enjoy it best straight out of the saucepan with its humble companion, the tortilla chip. Our favorites are the 365 Everyday Value Restaurant Style salted tortilla chips found at Whole Foods Market (Locate the Whole Foods Market nearest you).

This recipe is shamelessly reproduced from the incredible Isa Does It, a tome of vegan delights that eased my transition to vegetarianism.
